Welcome to Weston Moffat's CSD120 Website!

As a bright‐eyed student graduating from high school in 2013, I imagined myself as a successful lawyer, living it up in some city in the not so distant future. Law class was my favourite subject in high school, so naturally, I assumed this was the best course of action. Initially enrolled in political science at The University of Western Ontario, I quickly made the switch to philosophy after being introduced to the thought‐provoking ideas and arguments of political philosophers such as Locke and Mill. Lawyers deal with rational and logical arguments on a daily basis, and philosophy could aid me in developing these skills.

Studying philosophy provided me with a new worldview; a different lens to view life. It was in my second year of university that I determined I no longer wished to be a lawyer. If you asked me exactly what prompted this decision, I wouldn't be ablecto base it on one factor; rather a plethora of influences and circumstances. The question now arose: “What could I possibly do with a philosophy degree?” As intriguing and interesting that I find philosophy, I couldn't quite picture myself spending eight years in university to get my PhD and teach. While being a professor enticed me, I couldn't find it in myself to put so much of my life into that discipline. However, it will always remain a lifelong passion. Just not at the forefront of my life.

Maybe the most overwhelming factor of deciding not to become a lawyer, was my focus on the monetary gains it provided and not the passion I had for it. Thus, I now needed to establish where this passion was and how I can spend my life doing it. I took a year and and a half hiatius from my studies, and now, here I am: studying computer programming at Sault College. I concluded that computer science was the domain to focus my time on. After extensive research over that year, I honestly think I've found a discipline that I'll enjoy, without considering how much money I'll make in the end. Not saying that isn't a factor; merely not a necessity.

Beyond the scope of philosophy, and now computer science, I enjoy reading, writing, playing video games, and spending time with my dog. I grew up in Thessalon, not far from Sault Ste. Marie, and so I'm much closer to home than I was during my time at Western. I'm looking forward to my studies at Sault College, and hopefully Algoma University afterwards.
